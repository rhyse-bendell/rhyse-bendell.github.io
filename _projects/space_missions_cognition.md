## Project Overview
This project focuses on cognition, coordination, and decision quality in complex mission environments where teams and autonomous tools must work together under uncertainty. The work is part of a broader Minerva-related research program on team cognition for future space operations.

My contribution centers on designing simulation-based studies, building research infrastructure, and translating findings into practical guidance for mission planning and training.

## Key Contributions
- **Mars Colony macrocognition study:** Led and supported study design and execution focused on collaborative problem-solving in a Mars mission scenario.
- **MITM simulator development and use:** Built and refined the MITM simulation environment as a visible, reusable platform for experimentation.
- **Team process-loss study:** Investigated where coordination breaks down during mission tasks and how those losses impact team outcomes.

## Mars Colony Macrocognition Study
The Mars Colony study examines how teams build and maintain shared understanding while dealing with time pressure, changing constraints, and competing objectives. Rather than studying isolated decisions, this work looks at macrocognition in context: how teams sense, coordinate, adapt, and recover when conditions shift.

The study design emphasizes realistic mission dynamics so that results are useful beyond the lab. Current analyses focus on communication patterns, information flow, and the interaction between human roles and supportive AI systems.

### MITM Simulator (Mission-in-the-Loop Platform)
The MITM simulator is a core component of this research and the primary environment used to run mission-inspired team experiments. It provides a structured way to test how cognitive demands, task design, and support tools shape team performance.

In practice, MITM allows rapid scenario iteration, instrumentation of team behavior, and consistent comparison across experimental conditions. This makes it possible to evaluate not only outcomes, but also *how* teams reached those outcomes.

## Team Process-Loss Study
This line of work investigates process loss: the gap between potential team capability and actual team performance during demanding mission tasks. The analysis focuses on where and why collaboration degrades, including delays in coordination, fragmented information sharing, and role handoff friction.

Findings from this effort are being used to inform scenario design, team training priorities, and future decision-support concepts for long-duration missions.
