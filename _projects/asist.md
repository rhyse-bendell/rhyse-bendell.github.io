      <h2>Project Overview</h2>
      <p>Artificial Social Intelligence for Successful Teams (ASIST) is a DARPA program focused on developing AI systems that function as effective teammates by modeling human cognition, inferring intent, and supporting coordination in dynamic, high-consequence settings. The core objective is to build artificial social intelligence: agent capabilities that interpret human goals, anticipate behavior, and contribute to collaborative problem solving rather than acting as isolated tools.</p>
      <p>My work centered on the design, execution, and analysis of large-scale human-subject experiments evaluating AI-supported teams in complex simulated environments, including Minecraft-based urban search-and-rescue scenarios. Across these studies, I helped connect social and cognitive theory to measurable team behaviors and deployable AI support strategies.</p>

      <li>Led cross-institution collaboration with IHMC, CMU, MIT, and partner teams</li>
      <li>Translated social and cognitive theory into testable AI hypotheses</li>

    <h2>Technical Approach</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Human-subjects experimentation in simulated environments</li>
      <li>Minecraft-based urban search-and-rescue testbed</li>
      <li>Multimodal behavioral data collection (movement, communication, task events)</li>
      <li>Team cognition modeling (mental models and shared understanding)</li>
      <li>Intervention-based paradigms for AI teammate evaluation</li>
      <li>Spatial-temporal coordination analysis using the flocking framework</li>
    </ul>
