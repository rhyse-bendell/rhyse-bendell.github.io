## Project Overview
ASIST (Artificial Social Intelligence for Successful Teams) was a DARPA program focused on AI systems that can function as teammates rather than standalone tools. At UCF, this work was conducted under **Dr. Stephen Fiore** and **Dr. Florian Jentsch**, with an emphasis on translating social and team-cognition theory into testable experimental designs and deployable AI-support concepts.

My contributions spanned study design, analysis pipeline leadership, and cross-team coordination across multiple ASIST studies.

## UCF Role and Research Focus
A recurring ASIST distinction was between **taskwork** (completing mission actions) and **teamwork** (coordination, common ground, adaptive planning). UCF focused on the teamwork side of AI support, including:

- teammate and team profiling relevant to taskwork and teamwork potential,
- theory-to-measure translation for social and cognitive constructs,
- real-time analytics for coordination and adaptation,
- and empirical evaluation of intervention timing/content.

## My Role
- UCF team project manager and lead contributor across study operations
- Lead study designer for UCF's final ASIST experiment effort
- Directed core analysis workflows across UCF ASIST studies
- Developed the **flocking** approach for quantifying team coordination patterns
- Coordinated work with partner teams across the larger ASIST ecosystem

## Experimental Context
ASIST studies used Minecraft-based collaborative scenarios where teams had differentiated roles, interdependencies, and time pressure. These designs made teamwork measurable rather than incidental.

- **Study 3 (Urban Search-and-Rescue):** role-based team coordination, victim search/triage workflows, and AI support targeted at collaboration quality.
- **Study 4 (Bomb-Disposal Context):** planning-resource-task coupling under time pressure, with AI advisor interactions integrated into team communication.
