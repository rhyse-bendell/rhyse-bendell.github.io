## Short Summary
A structured human-in-the-loop workflow for using LLM systems to accelerate literature synthesis, evidence extraction, and early-stage research planning. The system improves speed while preserving researcher oversight for quality and interpretation.

## Problem
Research teams spend substantial time on repetitive synthesis tasks before higher-order analysis can begin.

## Context
Human-subjects and systems research often requires processing large literatures quickly, but quality can suffer when synthesis is rushed or inconsistently documented.

## My Role
I designed the workflow architecture, defined human review checkpoints, and evaluated how assistant-supported steps affected throughput and output quality.

## Approach
I built modular prompt and review pipelines for source triage, extraction, summarization, and structured note generation, with explicit validation loops for human decision-makers.

## Outcome
The workflow produced more consistent early-stage synthesis artifacts and reduced manual overhead for recurring review tasks.

## Why It Matters
This project demonstrates a practical model for combining AI speed with human scientific judgment in real research operations.
