## Project Overview
This project focuses on practical AI support for recurring research workflow tasks: literature triage, evidence extraction, synthesis drafting, and structured output generation. The goal is to increase throughput on early-stage evidence processing while preserving scientific accountability.

## Core Design Principle
The workflow is intentionally **human-in-the-loop**. AI assists with speed and formatting, while researchers retain authority for source acceptance, interpretation, and final claims.

## Workflow Components
- **Source triage support:** prioritize and cluster candidate papers for review
- **Structured extraction:** capture methods, outcomes, and limitations in consistent templates
- **Synthesis drafting:** generate first-pass comparative summaries for researcher revision
- **Auditability:** preserve prompts, intermediate artifacts, and revision traces
- **Approval gates:** require researcher sign-off before outputs are used in formal deliverables

## My Role
I designed the workflow structure, defined review checkpoints, and tested how different levels of AI assistance affected consistency and turnaround time for recurring synthesis tasks.

## Outcomes
- Reduced repeated manual effort in early-stage evidence processing
- Improved consistency of extraction and synthesis artifacts across iterations
- Maintained researcher oversight at each decision-critical stage

## Why It Matters
For research teams, AI support is most useful when it accelerates routine processing without replacing scientific judgment. This project demonstrates an operational model for that balance.
